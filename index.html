Talks

Bunny Basics; Selecting the Right Rabbit and Basic Care

Marcy Berman

Founder and Executive Director, SaveABunny, Inc.

Every rabbit is a sentient individual deserving of love and respect. They, like all beings, have the right to feel secure and loved, and to have as much freedom as possible to express themselves. Adopting, fostering, volunteering and caring for a rescued rabbit is a profound life-changing experience that benefits people, the animals and the community at large.

Learn about SaveABunny

10 Surprising Facts About Rabbits

Mark Hawthorne

Author of The Way of The Rabbit

Independent and resourceful, rabbits represent balance, rebirth, speed, fertility, resurrection, abundance, creativity, magic, and harmony. After spending two years researching his new book, The Way of the Rabbit, author Mark Hawthorne will share 10 of the most surprising things he learned about these remarkable animals.

When to Go to the Vet

Anne Martin, PhD

Executive Director, House Rabbit Society

Domestic rabbits are companion animals and should be afforded at least the same individual rights, level of care, and opportunity for longevity as commonly afforded to dogs and cats who live as human companions.

Learn about HRS

RHDV2 update: Learn about the new deadly rabbit virus

Dr Anthony A. Pilny

Veterinary Advisor for the House Rabbit Society and member of the RHDV2 Task Force

Dr. Pilny has lectured nationally and internationally about avian and exotic pet medicine and has published numerous scientific articles and book chapters on birds, rabbits, and other exotic species. Dr. Pilny is a member of the Association of Avian Veterinarians, Association of Exotic Mammal Veterinarians, Arizona Veterinary Medical Association, and the American Veterinary Medical Association. He currently serves as the consulting medical director of the House Rabbit Society and is a member of the RHDV2 task force. He also serves on the advisory boards of The Avian Welfare Coalition and One Earth Conservation and chair of the Welfare Committee for the Association of Avian Veterinarians.

Dr. Pilny currently works at Arizona Exotic Animal Hospital in Phoenix, Arizona and has devoted the last 19 years to exotic pet veterinary practice with a focus on animal welfare.
